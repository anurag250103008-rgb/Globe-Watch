Suicide Car Bombing in Pakistan’s Khyber Pakhtunkhwa Kills 16 — Islamabad Blames India, Again Without Evidence

Anurag Srivastav 29/06/2025

A devastating suicide car bomb attack targeted a security convoy in Pakistan’s volatile Khyber Pakhtunkhwa province on Saturday,28/06/2025, leaving 16 people dead, including 13 security personnel, and injuring several others.

According to officials, a vehicle packed with explosives rammed into a convoy near a military checkpoint in North Waziristan, a region long troubled by militancy and cross-border insurgency. The blast caused a powerful shockwave, setting multiple vehicles ablaze and damaging nearby structures.

Shortly after the incident, Pakistani authorities pointed fingers at India, suggesting foreign involvement behind the attack. As of now, no militant group has claimed responsibility, and investigations are ongoing. Still, Islamabad’s accusations follow a repetitive narrative — blaming India without offering verifiable evidence.

India has consistently denied such claims in the past and maintains that Pakistan must address the terror infrastructure operating within its borders rather than resorting to baseless allegations.

Key Facts:
Casualties: 16 dead (13 security personnel, 3 civilians), several injured

Location: Near a checkpoint in North Waziristan, Khyber Pakhtunkhwa

Method: Suicide car bombing targeting a military convoy

Pakistan’s claim: India involved — no proof presented

India’s stance: Yet to respond, but has previously called such claims "unfounded and diversionary"

The incident highlights Pakistan’s deteriorating internal security, especially in its tribal border regions, and raises renewed concerns over its ability to curb domestic extremism. As regional tensions persist, calls for a fact-based investigation grow louder — rather than turning complex internal issues into cross-border blame games.
