Disappointment and Disorder: Chaos Erupts During Lionel Messi's Kolkata Visit

A much-anticipated appearance by football icon Lionel Messi in Kolkata, India, as part of his 'GOAT Tour India 2025', descended into widespread chaos and fan vandalism on Saturday, December 13, 2025. The disorder at the Vivekananda Yuba Bharati Krirangan (Salt Lake Stadium) cast a severe shadow over what was meant to be a historic celebration of the sport.

The Source of the Frustration

The core issue stemmed from the extremely brief nature of Messi's public appearance and the poor management that severely restricted fans' ability to see the star.

  • Blink-and-Miss Appearance:

Despite a program scheduled to last for nearly two hours, Messi's presence at the stadium, accompanied by teammates Luis Suárez and Rodrigo De Paul, was reportedly cut short to less than 25 minutes.

  • Obstructed Views:

Fans, many of whom had paid steep prices for tickets (starting at around Rs 4,366), complained bitterly that they could not catch a clear glimpse of the football legend. Messi was immediately surrounded by a large entourage of security personnel, organisers, politicians, and local dignitaries who crowded the pitch, effectively blocking the view from the galleries.

 

  • Failed Expectations: 

The tight cordon and the star's early departure prevented the planned full lap of appreciation and curtailed interactions, leaving the tens of thousands of spectators deeply frustrated.

Vandalism and Police Intervention

As news of Messi's premature exit spread, the disappointment of the crowd escalated into open disorder.

 

  • Vandalism: Irate fans began hurling bottles, plastic chairs, and broken seats onto the field. They tore down sponsor banners and damaged temporary structures.

     

  • Breaching Security: Hundreds of fans broke through the perimeter fencing and surged onto the pitch, prompting the deployment of the Rapid Action Force (RAF).

     

  • Police Action: Police were forced to resort to baton charges (lathi-charge) to disperse the large, angry crowd that had taken control of the stadium floor.

  •  

    Official Fallout and Accountability

    The incident, which contrasted sharply with Messi's smooth and celebrated visit in 2011, prompted immediate reactions from authorities:

    • Chief Minister's Apology: West Bengal Chief Minister Mamata Banerjee publicly apologized to Lionel Messi and the disappointed fans, expressing shock over the "mismanagement." She ordered a high-level enquiry committee to investigate the incident and fix responsibility.

       
       

    • Organiser Detained: Police detained Satadru Dutta, the main organiser of the event, for alleged mismanagement and promised that full ticket refunds would be issued to the disappointed spectators.

       

    • Political Slugfest: Opposition parties heavily criticized the ruling government, alleging that ministers and party leaders prioritized photo opportunities over fan experience, leading to the chaos and the tarnishing of Kolkata's image as a global football hub.

       

    The turbulent scenes in Kolkata cast a pall over the start of the 'GOAT India Tour 2025', though subsequent legs of the tour in cities like Hyderabad proceeded without similar reports of chaos.
