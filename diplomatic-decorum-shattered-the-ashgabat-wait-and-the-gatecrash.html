Diplomatic Decorum Shattered: The Ashgabat Wait and the 'Gatecrash'

XERO 13/12/2025

Pakistani Prime Minister Shehbaz Sharif reportedly faced a diplomatic embarrassment in Ashgabat, Turkmenistan, after allegedly being left waiting by Russian President Vladimir Putin.

The incident, which occurred at the International Forum on Peace and Trust, culminated in what was widely described as Sharif's unscripted "gatecrash" of a closed-door meeting between Putin and Turkey's President Erdoğan.

The public perception of the event highlighted Russia's contrastingly warm ties with India, fueling claims of a significant diplomatic snub to Pakistan.

The 40-Minute Wait

According to initial reports and viral video footage shared by Russian state media outlet RT India (which later deleted the post with a clarification), Prime Minister Sharif and his delegation were scheduled for a meeting with President Putin but were allegedly kept waiting for over 40 minutes in a separate hall.

 

Video snippets from the time showed a visibly restless Sharif. The prolonged wait, which is often interpreted in diplomatic circles as a calculated slight or a demonstration of priority, set the stage for the controversial sequence of events that followed.

The Unscripted Entry

Patience, it seems, eventually wore thin. Reports indicate that Prime Minister Sharif, after the considerable delay, chose to enter an ongoing, closed-door bilateral meeting between President Putin and Turkish President Recep Tayyip Erdoğan.

 

This unscripted entry—dubbed a "gatecrash" by many commentators—is considered a severe breach of diplomatic protocol. While the exact conversation during his brief presence is unknown, Sharif reportedly stayed in the meeting for only about ten minutes before exiting. The optics of the unannounced intrusion, regardless of the cause, have been widely scrutinised as a desperate and undiplomatic manoeuvre.

Context and Contrasts

The diplomatic fallout of the Ashgabat incident is magnified by the current geopolitical environment:

  • Recent Putin-Modi Bonhomie:   

The Ashgabat event came shortly after President Putin's high-profile and conspicuously warm visit to India, where he engaged in private dinners and animated conversations with Prime Minister Narendra Modi. This contrast has been highlighted by analysts, suggesting a significant difference in Moscow's diplomatic comfort levels and priorities with New Delhi versus Islamabad.

  • Previous Embarrassments:

This is not the first time a Sharif-Putin meeting has drawn attention for an awkward moment. At an earlier Shanghai Cooperation Organisation (SCO) summit, Prime Minister Sharif struggled publicly with his translation headphones, an incident that also went viral and became a source of online ridicule.

Retraction and Official Communication

Despite the viral narrative, the Russian state media outlet RT India deleted its post, admitting a "misrepresentation of the events." The Russian embassy in Pakistan also released footage of a seemingly cordial handshake between Putin and Sharif. Nevertheless, the image of Sharif waiting and entering the closed meeting remains a powerful visual narrative, fueling claims of a deliberate diplomatic humiliation against Pakistan.
